Visualizing Women’s Advancement:
A Global View of Women’s Educational, Financial, and Social Wellbeing

By: Jose DelValle, Claudia Durkin, Lizzie Friend, Maria Manna, MaKenzie Muller

W231 Summer 2023

Introduction

This project aims to visualize the advancement of women globally in areas such as health, education, marriage, and labor & finance. The data used comes from The World Bank Gender Data Portal and U.S. Maternal Education datasets. The primary users of this information are public health leaders, policymakers, business investors, and the general public.

Objectives and Challenges

The goal is to gain insights on gender differences in health, education, marriage, and financial topics, globally and in the United States. We acknowledge the potential issues of data inequity, with missing data for lower-income countries and those with economic instability due to war, famine, and lasting impacts of the Covid-19 pandemic.

Indicators

The four main categories of indicators include healthcare (maternal ages and access to medical and prenatal care), education (years of schooling), marriage (access and equality in partnerships), and labor & finance (access to financial resources and jobs).

Healthcare

Findings suggest an increase in mothers' average age at childbirth, a decrease in maternal deaths due to improved healthcare, and a reduction in family size owing to the accessibility of contraceptives. However, regions like Sub Saharan Africa and South Asia are still lagging behind the rest of the world.

Education

The gap between female and male years of schooling has been closing globally. In some regions, females have overtaken males. East Asia has experienced the fastest increase in female years of schooling, overtaking Europe. However, post-COVID-19, trends reversed in South Asia. Females in Sub-Saharan Africa still lag males.

Labor & Finance

Rates of access to bank accounts for women have increased over time but are still lower than those for men. The improvement in women's rates relative to men's was less than expected.

Testing Plan and Next Steps

The team plans to conduct usability testing on a live, interactive prototype, using predetermined tasks/task scenarios. They aim to finalize the testing plan, combine visuals into four cohesive dashboards, one for each indicator, and refine dashboards based on usability testing feedback. They also plan to embed the final visuals into an interactive platform using scrolly-telling methods.
